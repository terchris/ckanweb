Getting started with open data

The getting started courses give you the basics about open data, how to publish and how to prepare data for publication.

If you are a project manager for a smart city project then this course series are for you.

What can open data do for me?

This is an introductory and inspirational course on what open data is and what can be achieved when data is made open to everyone.
We go through examples of apps and visualisations that use open data. We look at how data must be prepared so that it easy to use and how to publish it so that the data is available for everyone.
We also look at how hackathons and smart city weekend events inspire entrepreneurs and civic hackers to create solutions for citizens.
Takeaway: You get an overview of what can be achieved with open data, how to create inspiring events,
Who: Anyone who is new to open data

Introduction to CKAN

ckan.org is the de facto standard for publishing open data and is used by the biggest open data portals around the world. The EU data portal, UK Gov, US Gov, Barcelona, Singapore ++
CKAN is open source and free.
In this course we go through how to publish data from sources like MS Excel, CSV, … Then how to use the built in visualisation tools to display the data.
Takeaway: You get an overview of ckan and how to publish and visualize open data
Who: Anyone who is new to open data

Visualizing and combining open data

We look at tools for analyzing and visualizing open data from several sources. Eg how to combine data from traffic with pollution.
Takeaway: You get an overview of tools that can combine and visualize open data.
Who: Anyone who is new to open data

Open data and standards

We look at the importance of standards when publishing open data. We se how Tags and Groups are used to organize data so that it is simpler to find datasets.
Takeaway: You get an understanding of standards, witch ones that exist and where to find them
Who: Anyone who is new to open data

How to measure success with your open data project

Who is your “customer” for your open data project? How do you get our customer engaged. How do you define the KPIs for your open data project?
Takeaway: Be able to formulate who is the customer for your open data project and how to measure
Who: Anyone who is new to open data / strategy & project management
